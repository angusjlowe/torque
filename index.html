An Introduction to the Basics

The Equation for Torque

If you only take away one thing from all this it must necessarily be that torque is equal to distance from pivot times the weight, or as an equation: T = r X F. Remember this one equation, and you're golden for all torque-related questions. However, as usual, there's a bit more to this topic than meets the eye.

Examples of Torque in Nature

Which way will this plank of wood tip? The weight on the right side is heavier, but the weight on the left is further away, which also contributes to the torque since torque is distance from pivot times the weight. In other words, the left weight has a larger value for distance while the right one has a larger value of weight. Evaluating the formula for torque, we find that on the right the torque is 30 Nm while on the left it's 50 Nm. Therefore, the torque generated around the pivot due to the left weight is greater, and the plank of wood tips to the left. Neat!
